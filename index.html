Home & Auto

Buying a car and a home are two of the biggest purchases youll ever make, so we want to make sure you have all the information you need to get there. Dont feel pressured to reach these milestones right now. Were over here chilling on standby until you need us!

In the meantime, heres some information on auto and home loan options to get you started:

Auto Refi

Love your car but not your payment? An auto refinance, or auto refi, could be what youre looking for. With an auto refi, the lender will pay off your old auto loan and give you a new loan for the remaining balance on your vehicle. With a refi, you could potentially lower your payments and/or get a lower interest rate.

Four Reasons to Consider a Refi

1

LOWER INTEREST RATES:
If your credit score has improved since you first purchased your vehicle, you may be eligible for lower interest rates. Even if your credit score has remained stable, interest rates may have dropped. Theres no need to stick with your current lender unless youre getting the absolute best deal.

2

LOWER PAYMENTS:
You can lower your monthly payment amount on your current vehicle with a refinance by:

Extending your terms
(for example, if you have 14 months left you could get a 2- or 3-year auto refi loan),

Getting a lower interest rate OR

A combination of both.

3

FASTER REPAYMENT:
Purchase a new vehicle and youll likely have five or six years of payments ahead of you again. On the other hand, if you refinance your existing car, truck or SUV, you could opt for a shorter loan term without feeling a pinch in your wallet.

4

CASH BACK AND OTHER OFFERS:
Youre probably not getting anything special from your existing loan. Refinance your auto loan, though, and you may be able to snag a special deal that will put more money in your pocket.

Auto Loan

If youre in the market to buy a new or used car, truck or SUV, an auto loan can help you make your purchase. Here are the five major components that youll want to consider when buying a vehicle.

1

PURCHASE PRICE:
The sale price of the vehicle. Many dealerships will allow you to negotiate this amount.

2

DOWN PAYMENT:
Your lender may require a down payment on your vehicle; aim for 20% down, although some lenders will accept less.

3

LOAN AMOUNT:
Your loan amount would be the total cost of your vehicle + taxes & fees your down payment and any dealer incentives.

4

ANNUAL PERCENTAGE RATE (APR):
The APR is the percent of your loan balance that a lender charges you annually for the loan.

5

TERMS:
The length of your loan. Common terms include 60 and 72 months.

6

MONTHLY PAYMENT:
The amount you pay on your auto loan each month, which depends on all of the factors above. Auto loans typically have a fixed APR, which means your payment amount does not fluctuate.

GET PREQUALIFIED (OR PREAPPROVED) FOR A HOME LOAN:
When you make an offer on a home, youre required to either have a Prequalification Form (PQF) from a lender or disclose that you have not been prequalified. When you provide a prequalification, the seller knows that your income and assets have already been documented by the lender which means that youre more likely to get the financing you need to buy the home.

Prequalification vs. Preapproval

A prequalification estimates the loan amount you might be approved for, while preapproval tells you the specific loan amount youre approved for with that lender.

Most lenders will look at four factors when preapproving you for a loan: your income, monthly expenses, down payment amount and your credit history. To qualify for a home loan through Define Mortgage Solutions (a subsidiary of Desert Financial), you must:

Have a minimum credit score of 620 for conventional loans (lower credit scores may still qualify for FHA financing), and

Have a minimum down payment of 3 percent for conventional loans, although some buyers may qualify for down payment assistance programs.

After these steps come the heart of the homebuying process, which includes steps from making an offer to completing loan paperwork, scheduling a home inspection, negotiating and closing. Check out our free Homebuying Toolkit for step-by-step guides to finding and buying a home!

Home Equity Line of Credit

If you have equity in your current home, you could potentially borrow against that equity to have extra money for home upgrades or repairs, to pay bills or for another need. Equity is simply the difference between the value of your home and the amount you still owe on your mortgage.

A HELOC, or home equity line of credit, works similarly to a credit card. You may withdraw some or all of your HELOC funds as you need them during your draw period (typically, 5 to 10 years).

HELOCs typically have variable rates, which means your interest rate will fluctuate up and down with the market. Lenders such as Desert Financial offer a fixed-rate HELOC option which allows you to cement your interest rate when you withdraw funds.

Benefits of a HELOC include:

Flexible and easy-to-use

Withdraw funds only as you need them

Fixed-rate option available to help you plan your payments

This type of loan works well for situations where you may need the money in smaller increments over time for example, if youre planning to complete several remodeling projects in the coming years.

Wills and Trusts

A will is a legal document that outlines how someones personal assets will be divided after their death. It can also contain specific requests for funeral arrangements and determine who will care for children and other legal dependents if both parents/caretakers pass on.

A trust is similar to a will, except it goes into effect immediately (rather than after death). Trusts also detail how a persons assets will be divided; however, they dont include any instructions for funeral arrangements or guardianship.

A credit union may not be the first place that comes to mind when you think about making a will, but Desert Financial has a dedicated team that can walk you step-by-step through the process of creating a will or trust. We can also help you prepare other legal documents including property deeds, LLC paperwork and Financial and/or Medical Powers of Attorney (POA).

Credit Unions vs. Banks: Whats the Difference?

While both banks and credit unions help you manage your finances, their philosophies couldnt be more different!

A credit union is a not-for-profit, member-owned organization and cooperative. Credit unions can make a profit, but they reinvest that revenue into the organization to benefit members and give back to the community. The primary goal of a credit union is to help its members.

Banks pay out large dividends to their shareholders; whereas credit unions pay out dividends and other big benefits to their members. While the goal of a credit union is to meet its members needs with the best terms and customer service, banks strive instead to earn profits for their investors.

Personalized Service and Exceptional Experiences

Credit unions carry a reputation for developing personal relationships with their members so they can better meet their individual needs. As a local institution, credit unions care deeply about their community. Thats why they value personal engagement with their members. Credit unions want to help their community members achieve their goals, build their futures and live with financial stability.

Desert Financial is here to help you grow like we helped our longtime member, Harry, put down roots in Arizona.

Community Outreach

Many members enjoy being a part of an organization with a mission to do good within the community. Credit unions are passionate about donating to local charities and planning and participating in community service projects.
